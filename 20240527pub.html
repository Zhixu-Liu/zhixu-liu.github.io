A Corner of Courtyard

GAME-722: Real-time Materials and Shaders


Project Overview

  In this project, I designed and created a small landscape scene. In order to complete this project, I learned and utilized industry-standard PBR workflows. Moreover, out of personal interest, I challenged myself to create stylized, cartoon-like textures.
  Steps: Concept, Modeling, Texturing, Rendering

Concept Design

  Because of my appreciation for stylized works, I integrated the cartoon-like style into this scene.
  To achieve the desired style for the project and maintain consistency across all objects, I conducted extensive research and learning online. I looked for existing stylized works and inferred the production methods based on their final visual appearance.
  Through this project, I became familiar with the techniques for creating stylized textures and hope to innovate and realize more diverse styles in my future works.

Modeling and UV Mapping

  I used Maya for modeling and UV mapping.
  Based on the final artistic output, I chose to use the model structure to express some details, while using textures to convey other detailed elements.
  Through this project, I practiced and learned how to correctly split UV maps for different objects to ensure that textures are properly displayed on the models.

Textures and Materials

  I created the materials in Substance Designer. Using basic shapes and node functionalities, I produced various materials.
  When producing textures, my focus is on creating a watercolor brush texture. To achieve this, I primarily used the slope blur node.
  In Substance Painter, I adjusted some of the material's color channels by hand-painted to ensure stylistic and tonal consistency across different materials. Additionally, I modified some material seams to enhance the overall aesthetic level of the textures.

Rendering in Unreal Engine

  I utilized shaders and material applications within Unreal Engine. To make a nighttime ambiance, I used point lights as the primary light source, making the environment appear more like fields at night.
